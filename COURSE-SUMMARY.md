## Module 3: Probability Basics
**Duration**: ~10 hours | **Lessons**: 4

### Lesson 1: Introduction to Probability
-  Experiments, outcomes, sample space
-  Events (simple and compound)
-  Classical, Empirical, Subjective probability
-  Basic probability rules (0 ≤ P(A) ≤ 1)
-  Complement rule: P(A') = 1 - P(A)

### Lesson 2: Addition and Multiplication Rules
-  Addition rule for mutually exclusive events: P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B)
-  General addition rule: P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B)
-  Multiplication rule for independent events: P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B)
-  General multiplication rule: P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B|A)

### Lesson 3: Conditional Probability
-  Conditional probability: P(B|A) = P(A and B) / P(A)
-  Independence vs. dependence
-  Tree diagrams
-  Contingency tables and conditional probability

### Lesson 4: Counting and Combinations
-  Fundamental counting principle
-  Factorials (n!)
-  Permutations: P(n,r) = n!/(n-r)!
-  Combinations: C(n,r) = n!/[r!(n-r)!]
-  Applications to probability

**Assessment**: 5-question pre/post, 20 practice problems, 15-question quiz

## Module 4: Discrete Probability Distributions
**Duration**: ~10 hours | **Lessons**: 4

### Lesson 1: Random Variables
-  Definition of random variable
-  Discrete vs. Continuous random variables
-  Notation (X, x)

### Lesson 2: Discrete Probability Distributions
-  Probability distribution requirements (Σp = 1, 0 ≤ p ≤ 1)
-  Probability mass function (PMF)
-  **Expected value**: E(X) = Σ[x · P(X=x)]
-  **Variance**: Var(X) = Σ[(x - μ)² · P(X=x)]
-  Standard deviation: σ = √Var(X)
-  Real-world example: US household size distribution (2020 Census data)

### Lesson 3: Binomial Distribution
-  **FTIC conditions**: Fixed n, Two outcomes, Independent trials, Constant probability
-  Binomial formula: P(X = k) = C(n,k) × p^k × (1-p)^(n-k)
-  Mean: μ = np
-  Standard deviation: σ = √(np(1-p))
-  Using binomial tables

### Lesson 4: Applications of Binomial Distribution
-  Real-world problems
-  Normal approximation to binomial (when np ≥ 10 and n(1-p) ≥ 10)
-  Using technology (calculators, software)

**Assessment**: 5-question pre/post, 20 practice problems, 15-question quiz

## Module 5: Continuous Probability Distributions
**Duration**: ~10 hours | **Lessons**: 5

### Lesson 1: Introduction to Normal Distribution
-  Characteristics of normal distribution (bell curve, symmetric, μ and σ)
-  **Empirical Rule (68-95-99.7 Rule)**
-  Area under the curve = probability

### Lesson 2: Standard Normal Distribution
-  Z-scores: z = (x - μ) / σ
-  Standard normal distribution (μ = 0, σ = 1)
-  Using z-tables (Table A)
-  Finding areas/probabilities

### Lesson 3: Finding Probabilities with Normal Distribution
-  P(X < a), P(X > a), P(a < X < b)
-  Converting to z-scores
-  Using z-tables for probabilities
-  Technology (calculators, normalcdf)

### Lesson 4: Normal Approximation and Applications
-  Finding x-values from probabilities (inverse normal)
-  Percentiles
-  Real-world applications (heights, test scores, measurements)
-  Assessing normality

### Lesson 5: Uniform Distribution
-  Uniform distribution U(a,b)
-  PDF: f(x) = 1/(b-a)
-  Mean: μ = (a + b) / 2
-  Standard deviation: σ = (b - a) / √12
-  Probabilities: P(x₁ < X < x₂) = (x₂ - x₁) / (b - a)

**Assessment**: 5-question pre/post, 20 practice problems, 15-question quiz

## Module 6: Sampling Distributions
**Duration**: ~10 hours | **Lessons**: 4

### Lesson 1: Introduction to Sampling Distributions
-  Sampling distribution concept
-  Distribution of sample statistics
-  Sampling variability
-  Sampling distribution vs. population distribution

### Lesson 2: Central Limit Theorem & Sampling Distribution of Means
-  **Central Limit Theorem (CLT)**
-  **Sampling distribution of x̄**: μₓ̄ = μ
-  **Standard error**: σₓ̄ = σ/√n
-  Shape becomes normal as n increases (n ≥ 30 guideline)
-  Applications and calculations

### Lesson 3: Sampling Distribution of Proportions
-  Sample proportion: p̂ = x/n
-  Mean: μₚ̂ = p
-  Standard error: σₚ̂ = √(p(1-p)/n)
-  Normal approximation: np ≥ 10 and n(1-p) ≥ 10
-  Finding probabilities with sample proportions

### Lesson 4: Standard Error and Applications
-  Standard error vs. standard deviation
-  Effect of sample size on SE
-  Applications to real-world sampling
-  Confidence in estimates

## Module 7: Confidence Intervals & Sample Size
**Duration**: ~10 hours | **Lessons**: 4

### Lesson 1: Introduction to Confidence Intervals
-  Point estimate vs. interval estimate
-  Confidence level (C = 1 - α)
-  Margin of error
-  General form: estimate ± margin of error
-  Interpretation of confidence intervals

### Lesson 2: Confidence Intervals for Means
-  **When σ is known**: x̄ ± z* × (σ/√n)
-  **When σ is unknown**: x̄ ± t* × (s/√n)
-  **t-Distribution**: Properties, degrees of freedom (df = n - 1)
-  Using t-tables
-  When to use z vs. t

### Lesson 3: Confidence Intervals for Proportions
-  Formula: p̂ ± z* × √(p̂(1-p̂)/n)
-  Conditions: np̂ ≥ 10 and n(1-p̂) ≥ 10
-  Common confidence levels (90%, 95%, 99%)
-  Critical values

### Lesson 4: Sample Size Determination
-  **For means**: n = (z*σ / E)²
-  **For proportions**: n = p̂(1-p̂) × (z*/E)²
-  Conservative estimate for proportions: use p̂ = 0.5
-  Trade-offs: precision vs. cost

**Assessment**: 5-question pre/post, 20 practice problems, 15-question quiz

## Module 8: Hypothesis Testing (One Sample)
**Duration**: ~10 hours | **Lessons**: 4

### Lesson 1: Introduction to Hypothesis Testing
-  Null hypothesis (H₀) vs. Alternative hypothesis (Hₐ)
-  Test statistic
-  Significance level (α)
-  Critical region / Rejection region
-  One-tailed vs. Two-tailed tests
-  **6-step hypothesis testing procedure**

### Lesson 2: Type I and Type II Errors
-  **Type I error (α)**: Rejecting true H₀
-  **Type II error (β)**: Failing to reject false H₀
-  **Power of a test (1 - β)**
-  Relationship between α and β
-  Factors affecting power (n, effect size, α)
-  Real-world consequences

### Lesson 3: Hypothesis Tests for Means
-  **Z-test** (σ known): z = (x̄ - μ₀) / (σ/√n)
-  **T-test** (σ unknown): t = (x̄ - μ₀) / (s/√n)
-  Degrees of freedom: df = n - 1
-  **Critical value approach**
-  **P-value approach**
-  Complete examples

### Lesson 4: Hypothesis Tests for Proportions
-  One-sample proportion test
-  Test statistic: z = (p̂ - p₀) / √(p₀(1-p₀)/n)
-  Conditions: np₀ ≥ 10 and n(1-p₀) ≥ 10
-  Applications

**Assessment**: 5-question pre/post, 20 practice problems, 15-question quiz

## Module 9: Hypothesis Testing for Two Populations
**Duration**: ~10 hours | **Lessons**: 4

### Lesson 1: Two-Sample Tests for Means (Independent)
-  Independent vs. dependent samples
-  **Unpooled (Welch's t-test)**: t = (x̄₁ - x̄₂) / √(s₁²/n₁ + s₂²/n₂)
-  **Pooled variance approach**: sp² = [(n₁-1)s₁² + (n₂-1)s₂²] / (n₁ + n₂ - 2)
-  When to use pooled vs. unpooled
-  Degrees of freedom (Welch's approximation)

### Lesson 2: Paired Samples (Matched Pairs)
-  What are paired samples? (before/after, twins, matched)
-  Why use paired tests? (controls variability)
-  **Paired t-test**: t = (d̄ - μd₀) / (sd/√n)
-  Degrees of freedom: df = n - 1 (n = pairs)
-  Process: calculate differences, test mean difference

### Lesson 3: Two-Sample Tests for Proportions
-  Comparing two proportions
-  **Pooled proportion**: p̄ = (x₁ + x₂) / (n₁ + n₂)
-  Test statistic: z = (p̂₁ - p̂₂) / √(p̄(1-p̄)(1/n₁ + 1/n₂))
-  Conditions for both samples
-  Important: pool for tests, NOT for CIs

### Lesson 4: Choosing the Right Test
-  Decision flowchart
-  Independent vs. paired
-  Pooled vs. unpooled
-  Common mistakes

**Assessment**: 5-question pre/post, 20 practice problems, 15-question quiz

## Module 10: Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)
**Duration**: ~10 hours | **Lessons**: 4

### Lesson 1: Introduction to ANOVA
-  What is ANOVA?
-  **Why not multiple t-tests?** α inflation problem
-  Between vs. within group variability
-  **F-distribution**
-  **F-statistic**: F = MSB / MSW
-  Hypotheses: H₀: μ₁ = μ₂ = μ₃ = ...

### Lesson 2: One-Way ANOVA Procedure
-  **Sum of Squares**:
  - SST (Total Sum of Squares)
  - SSB (Between Sum of Squares)
  - SSW (Within Sum of Squares)
  - Relationship: SST = SSB + SSW
-  **Degrees of freedom**: df_between = k-1, df_within = N-k
-  **Mean Squares**: MSB = SSB/df_between, MSW = SSW/df_within
-  **ANOVA table** construction
-  Complete worked example

### Lesson 3: Post-Hoc Tests
-  Why post-hoc tests? (ANOVA doesn't tell which means differ)
-  **Tukey's HSD (Honestly Significant Difference)**
-  **Bonferroni correction**: α_adjusted = α / number of comparisons
-  Other tests: Scheffé, Dunnett
-  Only do post-hoc if ANOVA is significant!

### Lesson 4: ANOVA Assumptions and Conditions
-  **Three key assumptions**:
  1. Independence
  2. Normality
  3. Equal variances (homogeneity)
-  Checking assumptions
-  Robustness
-  What if violated? (Kruskal-Wallis, Welch's ANOVA)
-  When to use ANOVA vs. other tests

**Assessment**: 5-question pre/post, 20 practice problems, 15-question quiz

## Module 11: Simple Linear Regression
**Duration**: ~10 hours | **Lessons**: 4

### Lesson 1: Introduction to Linear Regression
-  Bivariate quantitative data
-  Scatter plots (direction, form, strength)
-  **Correlation coefficient (r)**: -1 ≤ r ≤ 1
-  Properties of correlation
-  **Correlation ≠ Causation**
-  Explanatory vs. response variable

### Lesson 2: The Regression Equation
-  **Simple linear regression**: ŷ = b₀ + b₁x
-  Least squares method
-  **Slope**: b₁ = r × (sy / sx)
-  **Intercept**: b₀ = ȳ - b₁x̄
-  **Residuals**: y - ŷ
-  **Standard error**: se = √[Σ(y - ŷ)² / (n - 2)]
-  **Coefficient of determination (r²)**: proportion of variation explained
-  Predictions (interpolation vs. extrapolation)

### Lesson 3: Hypothesis Testing in Regression
-  **Testing correlation (ρ)**: t = r√[(n-2)/(1-r²)]
-  **Testing slope (β₁)**: t = b₁ / SE(b₁)
-  Equivalence of the two tests
-  **LINE conditions**: Linearity, Independence, Normality, Equal variance
-  Residual plots

### Lesson 4: Confidence and Prediction Intervals
-  **CI for slope**: b₁ ± t* × SE(b₁)
-  **CI for mean response**: ŷ ± t* × SE(ŷ_mean)
-  **Prediction interval for individual**: ŷ ± t* × SE(ŷ_ind)
-  Note the "1+" in PI formula
-  PI always wider than CI

**Assessment**: 5-question pre/post, 20 practice problems, 15-question quiz

## Module 12: Chi-Square Tests
**Duration**: ~10 hours | **Lessons**: 4

### Lesson 1: Introduction & Goodness of Fit
-  Chi-square (χ²) distribution
-  **Goodness of Fit Test**
-  Test statistic: χ² = Σ[(O - E)² / E]
-  Expected frequencies: E = n × p
-  Degrees of freedom: df = k - 1
-  Condition: All E ≥ 5

### Lesson 2: Test of Independence
-  Contingency tables (two-way tables)
-  Testing independence of two categorical variables
-  Expected frequencies: E = (RT × CT) / GT
-  Degrees of freedom: df = (r - 1)(c - 1)
-  Examples: 2×2, 2×3, 3×3 tables

### Lesson 3: Test of Homogeneity
-  Comparing distributions across populations
-  **Key difference from independence**: Multiple samples vs. one sample
-  Same calculations, different design
-  Examples comparing 3+ groups

### Lesson 4: Choosing the Right Chi-Square Test
-  Decision flowchart
-  Comparison table (goodness of fit vs. independence vs. homogeneity)
-  **Conditions**: Random sampling, independence, all E ≥ 5
-  Chi-square vs. other tests
-  Common mistakes

**Assessment**: 5-question pre/post, 20 practice problems, 15-question quiz
