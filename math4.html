About This Course

College Algebra for STEM students provides a rigorous foundation in algebraic concepts and techniques essential for success in calculus, statistics, physics, engineering, and other STEM courses. This course emphasizes functions, their properties, and real-world applications in science and technology.

About Math 4C:

Math 4C is a co-requisite course reviewing core algebraic concepts that support success in Math 4. Both courses must be taken concurrently.

This course can be challenging, both in terms of the breadth of the subject matter and its complexity. Almost every student will be challenged by course material at some point this term. Students who are most successful attend class consistently, complete all assignments, thoughtfully review feedback, develop good study strategies, and take advantage of academic support resources.

If you are having a hard time, please do not wait until the end of the term to seek guidance. I will be in a much better position to support your learning if you seek support early.

Topics Covered

  • Algebra fundamentals, relations, and functions
  • Solving equations and inequalities
  • Polynomial, rational, exponential, and logarithmic functions
  • Systems of equations and matrices
  • Analytic geometry and conic sections
  • Sequences, series, binomial theorem, and mathematical induction

Student Success

Tips for Success in Algebra

  • Practice daily: Mathematics is a skill that improves with consistent practice
  • Show your work: Partial credit is given for correct process, even if final answer is wrong
  • Master prerequisites: Algebra builds on basic math—review if needed
  • Use multiple resources: Textbook, videos, tutoring, office hours
  • Don't wait to get help: Address confusion early before falling behind
  • Connect concepts: Look for patterns and relationships between topics
