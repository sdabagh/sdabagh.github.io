## EMAIL BEST PRACTICES

### When Sending Recruitment Emails:

1. **Personalize when possible**: Use recipient's name, reference their institution
2. **Be transparent**: Clearly explain research purpose and voluntary nature
3. **Emphasize benefits**: Free course, AI tutor, flexible learning
4. **Respect privacy**: Make it clear participation is anonymous
5. **Provide contact info**: Make yourself accessible for questions
6. **Follow up appropriately**: Don't spam, but a gentle reminder is okay
7. **Respect opt-out requests**: Honor requests to be removed from communications

### Timing Recommendations:

- **Best time to recruit**: Start of semester (students planning ahead)
- **Best days**: Tuesday-Thursday (avoid Monday/Friday)
- **Best time**: Mid-morning (9-11 AM) or early evening (6-8 PM)
- **Follow-up**: 1-2 weeks after initial contact, then stop

### Ethical Considerations:

- Never pressure students to participate
- Make voluntary nature abundantly clear
- Don't offer excessive incentives (current = free course is appropriate)
- Be transparent about time commitment
- Respect faculty boundaries when recruiting through instructors
