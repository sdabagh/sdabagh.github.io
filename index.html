Dissertation Research

AI-Powered Cognitive Scaffolding for Statistics Education

My PhD research investigates how AI-powered cognitive scaffolding systems can improve learning outcomes in introductory statistics, with particular attention to diverse student populations across different institutional contexts.

I'm building a free online statistics learning platform that serves as both an educational resource and a research instrument, allowing me to rigorously study how AI tutoring affects learning, engagement, and educational equity.

Teaching Experience

I teach mathematics with a main focus on statistics at multiple institutions:

  • Santa Monica College (2020-Present): Math 54 (Introductory Statistics) and Math 4/4C (College Algebra)
  • West Los Angeles College (Ongoing): Mathematics courses
  • Loyola Marymount University (Current): Applied Statistics in Biology
  • UCLA: Summer courses in mathematics and statistics

Working with diverse learners across different institutional contexts directly informs my dissertation research, allowing me to understand the real challenges students face in learning statistics and to design AI-powered interventions that truly meet their needs.

Teaching Philosophy

I believe that every student can succeed in mathematics with the right support and tools. My teaching philosophy is rooted in compassion, clarity, and empowerment.

  • Inclusive environment: Students feel safe to ask questions, make mistakes, and grow
  • Real-world connections: Concepts linked to contexts students care about
  • Technology-enhanced: Tools that support diverse learning styles
  • Equity-focused: Increasing access to statistics and data literacy for all learners
  • Student-centered: Meeting learners where they are and supporting their journey

My Story

I'm a Lebanese-American mom of five who came to the U.S. determined to build a new life. I started at community college, transferred to UCLA, and earned degrees in Math/Economics and Statistics.

My journey wasn't linear—I paused my PhD when I lost my mother. But now, more than ever, I'm committed to completing my dissertation and using my research to help students like those I teach every day across multiple institutions.

My children—Adam, Aya, Abed, Issa, and Zachariah—are my greatest inspiration. I even run a home coding camp for my youngest two!
