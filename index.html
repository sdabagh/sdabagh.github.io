Research

Dissertation Research

AI-Powered Cognitive Scaffolding for Statistics Education

My PhD research investigates how AI-powered cognitive scaffolding systems can improve learning outcomes in introductory statistics, with particular attention to diverse student populations across different institutional contexts.

I'm building a free online statistics learning platform that serves as both an educational resource and a research instrument — studying how AI tutoring affects learning, engagement, and educational equity.

About Me

My Story

I'm a Lebanese-American mom of five who came to the U.S. determined to build a new life. I started at community college, transferred to UCLA, and earned degrees in Math/Economics and Statistics. My journey wasn't linear — I paused my PhD when I lost my mother. But now, more than ever, I'm committed to finishing my dissertation and using my research to help students like those I teach every day.

I teach at Santa Monica College, West Los Angeles College, Loyola Marymount University, and UCLA. My children — Adam, Aya, Abed, Issa, and Zachariah — are my greatest inspiration. I even run a home coding camp for my youngest two!
