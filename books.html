Published Textbooks

You're Smarter Than You Think

College Algebra for STEM Majors | 282 Pages

A comprehensive college algebra textbook designed specifically for STEM-track students at community colleges. This book takes a patient, step-by-step approach to building algebraic skills, emphasizing real-world applications and conceptual understanding alongside procedural fluency.

Topics include: Functions and their graphs, linear and quadratic functions, polynomial and rational functions, exponential and logarithmic functions, systems of equations, sequences, and applications.

Written for: Students at Santa Monica College, West LA College, and any student preparing for calculus and STEM coursework.

Statistics in the Eye of a Nurse

Statistics for Healthcare Professionals | 318 Pages

An introductory statistics textbook that teaches statistical concepts through the lens of healthcare and nursing. Every example, dataset, and application connects statistics to real clinical scenarios -- making the subject meaningful and relevant for students pursuing careers in healthcare.

Topics include: Data collection and study design, descriptive statistics, probability, normal distributions, confidence intervals, hypothesis testing, correlation and regression, and chi-square tests -- all through healthcare examples.

Written for: Nursing students, pre-health majors, and anyone learning statistics who wants healthcare-focused content.

About These Books

Both textbooks grew out of years of teaching at community colleges and seeing students struggle with expensive, one-size-fits-all textbooks that didn't speak to their experiences or goals. I wrote these books to be:

  • Free and accessible: No student should be priced out of learning
  • Clear and patient: Written the way I teach -- step by step, with plenty of examples
  • Relevant: Real-world contexts that connect to students' lives and career paths
  • Complete: Everything needed for a full course, from concept introductions to practice problems

If you're an instructor interested in adopting either book for your course, I'd love to hear from you: dabagh_safaa@smc.edu
