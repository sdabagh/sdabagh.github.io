🌿 About Me

Hi, I'm Safaa Dabagh — a Lebanese-American mom of five, math instructor, and lifelong learner.

I came to the U.S. with my three eldest children, determined to build a new life. I started at West Los Angeles College, then transferred to UCLA, where I earned a bachelor’s degree in Math/Economics and a minor in Statistics.

After completing my undergraduate studies, I continued on to earn my master’s degree in Statistics from UCLA and began my PhD. My doctoral journey was interrupted when I lost my mother, but now more than ever, I am committed to completing my dissertation and research.

Raising a large family while teaching and pursuing higher education has shaped my outlook on resilience, growth, and empathy. My children — Adam, Aya, Abed, Issa, and Zachariah — are my greatest inspiration and the heart of my journey.

I see myself as a student of life: always evolving, always curious. Whether I’m guiding students, parenting, or researching how AI can transform education, I lead with purpose, kindness, and determination.
