Workbook — Session 36: My 'Where I Went Today' Diary

Name: _________________________________

Date: _________________________________

Print this page. Use it during the session. Keep it in your folder.

🟢 1. Letter review — write your three favorites

You've learned all 28 Arabic letters! Pick three letters you love (or three that are still tricky) and write each one three times.

My letter Try 1 Try 2 Try 3

Star ⭐ the one that came out best.

🟡 2. Find the place words

Circle every word below that is a place you can go:

السّوق     قِطّة     الجَنينة     أَحمَر     بَيت تيتا     شَمس     البَحر

Hint: four of them are places. Three are not.

🟠 3. I can read these words

Read each word out loud. Point to it as you say it:

Picture Arabic Say it What it means
❓
وَين؟
 wayn? where?
👣
رِحت
 riht I went
🌅
بُكرا
 bukra tomorrow

Now put them together. Read out loud:

وَين رِحت اليَوم؟

wayn riht il-yawm? — Where did I go today?

🟣 4. My week — match the day to the place

Draw a line from each day to a place you might have gone (or pretend!).

Day Place
التَّنين (Monday)
 ─ →
السّوق (the souk)
التَّلات (Tuesday)
 ─ →
الجَنينة (the park)
الأَربَع (Wednesday)
 ─ →
بَيت صاحبي (friend's house)
الخَميس (Thursday)
 ─ →
بَيت تيتا (teta's house)

There's no wrong answer — match what feels true for your week.

🌟 6. Mini-challenge (optional stretch)

Can you write a whole tiny sentence about tomorrow? Use بُكرا.

بُكرا رَح روح ع _________________

(Tomorrow I'm going to _________________)

Read it to someone at home. Tell them: "I just wrote a sentence about the future in Arabic."
