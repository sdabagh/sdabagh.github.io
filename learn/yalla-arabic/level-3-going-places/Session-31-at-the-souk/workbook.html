🟢 1. Today's letter — ق (qaf)

The ق has a deep sound — way back in your throat. Like a "k" but deeper. Try it: qaf, qaf, qaf.

Trace each qaf. Notice the two dots on top and the round bowl that dips below the line.

ق . . . . ق . . . . ق . . . . ق . . . . ق

Now try writing one by yourself:

🟡 2. Find the qaf

Circle every word that has the letter ق (qaf) in it:

سوق     قِرش     غالي     قِنطار     رَخيص     بِكَم

Hint: four of them have a qaf. Two don't.

🟠 3. I can read these words

Read these three words out loud. Point to each one as you say it:

Picture Arabic Say it Meaning
🏪
سوق
 suuq market
💰
بِكَم؟
 bi-kam how much?
🪙
قِرش
 qirsh a small coin

After you read them, ask a grown-up to be the shopkeeper. You point at something in the room and ask:

بِكَم؟

🟣 4. Sort it: غالي or رَخيص?

In the souk, some things cost a lot (غالي — expensive) and some things cost a little (رَخيص — cheap). Draw a line from each item to where it belongs.

Item Probably...

غالي (expensive)

Some answers depend on where you live! Talk it out.

🌟 6. Mini-challenge (optional stretch)

Play souk with a grown-up. They pick something in your kitchen — a tomato, an apple, a piece of bread. You ask:

**بِكَم؟**

They tell you a number of قِرش. You decide: is it غالي or رَخيص? Then say it!
