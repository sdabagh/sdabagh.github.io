🟢 1. Today's letter — ح (Ha)

Trace each ḥa. Start at the top. Curve around like a little smile, then close it up.

ح . . . . ح . . . . ح . . . . ح . . . . ح

Now try writing one by yourself:

Big ḥa! Make it huge:

The sound is like a warm breath on a cold window — haaaa. Try it on your hand!

🟡 2. Find the ḥa

Circle every word that has the letter ح (ḥa) in it:

حُلو     كبير     حُلوة     صَغير     مَرحَبا

Hint: three of them have a ḥa. Did you find all three?

🌟 6. Mini-challenge (optional stretch)

Adjectives in Arabic change for boys and girls. Boy words usually end in a regular sound. Girl words usually add an -eh at the end.

Fill in the missing word:

Boy form (m) Girl form (f)
كبير
(kbir)
كبيرة
(kbireh)
صَغير
(saghir)
_____________
طَويل
(tawil)
_____________
حُلو
(hilu)
_____________

See the pattern? Add that little -eh sound and the word becomes a girl word.
