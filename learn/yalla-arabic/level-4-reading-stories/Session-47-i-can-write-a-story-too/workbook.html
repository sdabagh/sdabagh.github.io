🟡 2. Find the story words

Circle every word below that is a story-building word (a piece you need to write a story):

البِداية     تُفّاحة     الوَسَط     سَيّارة     النِّهاية     بَطَل     قِصَّتي

Hint: five of them belong to a story. Two are just random words!

🟠 3. I can read these words

Read these three words out loud. Point to each one as you say it:

Meaning Arabic Say it
I write
أَكتُب
 ak-tub
My story
قِصَّتي
 qis-sa-ti
Main character
بَطَل / بَطَلة
 ba-tal / ba-ta-leh

Now say the whole sentence:

أَكتُب قِصَّتي — I write my story.

🟣 4. Sort the story pieces

Every story has three parts. Draw a line from each Arabic word to the right part of the story.

Arabic What it means
البِداية
 ─ → 🏁 The end — how things finish
الوَسَط
 ─ → 🌱 The beginning — where we meet the character
النِّهاية
 ─ → 🎢 The middle — when something happens

🌟 6. Mini-challenge (optional stretch)

Write one full Arabic sentence about your story. Use words you already know. It can be tiny — that's okay!

Some sentence starters you can borrow:

أَكتُب قِصّة عَن...    (I write a story about...)

البَطَل اسمُه...    (The hero's name is...)

في النِّهاية...    (In the end...)
