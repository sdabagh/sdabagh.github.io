🟢 1. Today's letter — ك (kaf)

Trace each kaf. Start at the top corner, pull down, then across the bottom.

ك . . . . ك . . . . ك . . . . ك . . . . ك

Big kaf! Make it huge:

Tip: the little kaf-shape inside (the ء-looking mark) is what tells your eye "this is a kaf, not a lam."

🟡 2. Find the kaf

Circle every word that has the letter ك (kaf) in it:

كَم     مِن     مَعَك     لا     كِتاب     في     كَيف

Hint: four of them have a kaf. Look carefully — kaf can hide at the end of a word too!

🟠 3. I can read these words

Read each two-letter word out loud. Point to each letter as you say it. Then read the whole word smoothly.

Arabic Say it Means
في
 fee in
مِن
 min from
مَع
 ma'a with
كَم
 kam how much

Now read them faster. Then faster again. Two letters → one quick word!

🌟 6. Mini-challenge (optional stretch)

Build a tiny question! Put هَل (the question word) in front of a sentence to ask a yes/no question, or use كَم to ask "how much / how many."

Try reading these out loud:

هَل مَعَك كِتاب؟    (Do you have a book with you?)

كَم تُفّاحة؟    (How many apples?)

Now make your own question. Pick هَل or كَم and ask someone in your home:
