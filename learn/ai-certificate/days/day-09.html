Day 9 · 15 minutes

Manus

Delegate a multi-step task instead of asking a question.

~15 min read + 10 min hands-on

1 Why this tool matters

Manus represents the next tier: an agent, not a chatbot. You give it a goal, it plans the steps, opens tabs, reads pages, writes notes, and comes back with a deliverable. It is slow, sometimes wrong, and genuinely astonishing when it works. Today is about understanding the shape of this new kind of tool.

2 Walkthrough

  1. Go to manus.im and request access if needed (the waitlist moves fast).
  2. Give it a task: Research three electric SUVs in the $50-70k range that are available in California today. Build me a comparison table covering range, charging speed, cargo space, and tax credit eligibility. Cite every number.
  3. Watch the live trace: it searches, opens pages, compares data.
  4. When it finishes, review the table. Ask follow-up questions to refine.
  5. Notice the times it made mistakes. That is the cost of this tier of tool right now.

3 Your turn

Today’s exercise

Delegate one multi-hour research task you have been dreading — comparing health plans, researching vendors, drafting a competitive analysis. Let Manus take a first pass. You review and refine. Note how long you saved.

4 Pro tip

Worth keeping

Agents work best when the success criterion is obvious (a table, a list, a ranked shortlist). Vague goals like “help me think” are a job for ChatGPT, not Manus.
