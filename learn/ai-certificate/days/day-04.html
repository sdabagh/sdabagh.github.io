Day 4 · 15 minutes

Perplexity

Get a cited, verifiable answer instead of ten blue links.

~15 min read + 10 min hands-on

1 Why this tool matters

Perplexity is the tool you reach for when you want a cited answer fast. It searches the web in real time and writes a short synthesis with sources attached to every claim. It is less opinionated than ChatGPT and more focused than Google — a middle ground that is often exactly right for work questions.

2 Walkthrough

  1. Go to perplexity.ai. No account required to start.
  2. Ask: What are the main differences between HSAs and FSAs for a US employee choosing benefits this year?
  3. Read the answer. Each claim has a numbered source you can click.
  4. Try one of the follow-up suggestions at the bottom.
  5. Toggle Focus → Academic and re-ask a research question to see peer-reviewed sources only.

3 Your turn

Today’s exercise

Use Perplexity to answer a factual question you have been meaning to look up — a regulation, a historical fact, a comparison between two products. Open two of the cited sources to confirm. Save the conversation; Perplexity keeps a searchable history.

4 Pro tip

Worth keeping

Perplexity Spaces let you pin a set of trusted sources (your company wiki, a specific set of journals) and ask questions scoped to only those. Worth the 2 minutes to set up one.
