A Hayya Beena Naqraa story · Tier 0 (Whisper of the Alphabet · هَمْسَةُ الأَبْجَدِيَّة) · For ages 3–5

A pre-reader picture book. One letter, one word, one small picture per page. Read it slowly. Point to the letter. Let the child say the sound.

🗣️ Talk about it (for parent or teacher)

These aren't quiz questions. Ask one, point at the picture, and listen.

  1. Which picture was your favorite? Why that one?
  2. Can you find something in your house that starts with one of these letters? A book? Some bread? A glass of water?
  3. Let's pick one letter to say together five times. Which one?

✏️ Try it (a tiny activity, 2 minutes)

Pick one:

  • Trace one letter with your finger in the air. The grown-up traces it on your back. Switch.
  • Go on a tiny letter hunt. Walk through one room and find something — anything — that starts with one of these letters in Arabic. Kitab (book)? Ma' (water)? Khubz (bread)?
  • Pick your favorite picture in the book. Draw your own version on paper.

A note for grown-ups reading along

This is a pre-reader book — designed for ages 3 to 5, before children can decode letters on their own. You are the reader. Your child is the listener and the looker.

How to read it:

  1. Point to the big Arabic letter at the top of the page.
  2. Say the letter slowly: "Alif."
  3. Point to the word: "Arnab."
  4. Point to the picture: "Rabbit. Arnab."
  5. That's it. Move on. Don't quiz.

Repetition is the curriculum. Read this book over and over. Within a week or two, your child will start saying arnab before you do. That is the goal — not memorization, not testing, but the warmth of letters becoming friends.

A note on letter forms: Arabic letters change shape depending on where they sit in a word (beginning, middle, end). For now, we only show the simple isolated form. The other forms come gently in Tier 1.
