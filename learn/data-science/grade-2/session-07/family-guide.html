Session 7 Family Guide

Data Science for Young Minds — Tips for families facilitating Session 7: What Does the Data Say?

Overview

Learn to look at your graphs and say what you found. Practice 'The data shows that...'

Facilitating Each Lesson

Lesson 1: Data Tells a Story

Tip: Every graph tells a story. Learn to see the story hidden in the bars, heights, and patterns. Take time to let your child explore and share their thoughts.
Try asking: "What do you think about understanding that data tells a story?"

Lesson 2: The Data Shows...

Tip: Practice the most important sentence in data science: 'The data shows that...' Use it for everything! Take time to let your child explore and share their thoughts.
Try asking: "What do you think about using 'the data shows that...' sentence frame?"

Lesson 3: Fact vs. Opinion

Tip: The data shows facts. 'Pizza is the best' is YOUR opinion. Learn to tell the difference. Take time to let your child explore and share their thoughts.
Try asking: "What do you think about distinguishing between data facts and personal opinions?"

Lesson 4: Show and Tell

Tip: Present a graph to your family using the sentence frame: 'I asked ___, the data shows ___, I learned ___.'' Take time to let your child explore and share their thoughts.
Try asking: "What do you think about presenting data findings clearly to others?"

Conversation Starters

  • "What did you learn about understanding that data tells a story?"
  • "What did you learn about using 'the data shows that...' sentence frame?"
  • "What did you learn about distinguishing between data facts and personal opinions?"
  • "What did you learn about presenting data findings clearly to others?"
