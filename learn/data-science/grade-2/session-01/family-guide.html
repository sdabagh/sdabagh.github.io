Session 1 Family Guide

Data Science for Young Minds — Tips for families facilitating Session 1: Sorting by Two Things

Overview

Level up from Grade 1: sort objects by two attributes at once. Big AND red. Small AND blue.

Facilitating Each Lesson

Lesson 1: Review: One-Attribute Sorting

Tip: Remember Grade 1? We sorted by color, size, or shape. Let us review and get ready for the next level. Take time to let your child explore and share their thoughts.
Try asking: "What do you think about reviewing one-attribute sorting from grade 1?"

Lesson 2: Two Attributes at Once

Tip: What if something is big AND red? Sort objects using two attributes at the same time. Take time to let your child explore and share their thoughts.
Try asking: "What do you think about sorting by two attributes simultaneously?"

Lesson 3: Making a Sorting Grid

Tip: Create a grid with one attribute on each side. Place objects in the right square. See all combinations! Take time to let your child explore and share their thoughts.
Try asking: "What do you think about creating and using a sorting grid?"

Lesson 4: Empty Squares and Surprises

Tip: Some grid squares might be empty. That is data too! What does an empty square tell us? Take time to let your child explore and share their thoughts.
Try asking: "What do you think about understanding what empty categories mean?"

Conversation Starters

  • "What did you learn about reviewing one-attribute sorting from grade 1?"
  • "What did you learn about sorting by two attributes simultaneously?"
  • "What did you learn about creating and using a sorting grid?"
  • "What did you learn about understanding what empty categories mean?"
