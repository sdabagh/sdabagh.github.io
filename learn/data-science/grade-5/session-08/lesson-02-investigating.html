Check Your Understanding

1. How do you find the original source?

Answer: Follow the chain: the news article cites a study, the study has a methods section. Always go back to the original data, not just someone's summary of it.

2. What should you check about methodology?

Answer: Sample size, how participants were selected, what was measured, who funded the study, and when it was conducted. These details reveal whether the results are trustworthy.

3. What types of bias should you look for?

Answer: Selection bias (who was included/excluded), presentation bias (misleading graphs), framing bias (how questions were asked), and funding bias (who paid for it and what did they want to find).

4. What is often missing from data claims?

Answer: Context, sample size, margin of error, time frame, methodology details, and alternative explanations. What is NOT said is often more important than what is said.
