Session 5 Family Guide

Data Science for Young Minds — Tips for families facilitating Session 5: Variability: Why Answers Change

Overview

Why do you not get the same answer every time? Learn about variability, spread, and what is normal variation vs. meaningful change.

Facilitating Each Lesson

Lesson 1: Nothing Stays Exactly the Same

Tip: Discover that repeated measurements always vary. This is normal and expected. Take time to let your child explore and share their thoughts.
Try asking: "What do you think about variability is natural: temperatures fluctuate, test scores differ, measurements vary?"

Lesson 2: Measuring Spread: Range and More

Tip: Learn to measure how spread out data is using range and other tools. Take time to let your child explore and share their thoughts.
Try asking: "What do you think about range: the simplest measure of spread (max - min)?"

Lesson 3: Normal Variation vs. Meaningful Change

Tip: The hardest question in data science: is this change real or just normal variation? Take time to let your child explore and share their thoughts.
Try asking: "What do you think about what is normal day-to-day variation?"

Lesson 4: Variability All Around You

Tip: Find and measure variability in your daily life. Track something and discover your personal 'normal range.' Take time to let your child explore and share their thoughts.
Try asking: "What do you think about things that vary in your life: sleep, steps, mood, grades, weather?"

Conversation Starters

  • "What did you learn about variability is natural: temperatures fluctuate, test scores differ, measurements vary?"
  • "What did you learn about range: the simplest measure of spread (max - min)?"
  • "What did you learn about what is normal day-to-day variation?"
  • "What did you learn about things that vary in your life: sleep, steps, mood, grades, weather?"
