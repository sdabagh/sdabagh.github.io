Check Your Understanding

1. What should you do when data contradicts what you expected?

Answer: Trust the data, not your assumptions. Check for errors first, but if the data is correct, your assumption was wrong — and that is how you learn.

2. What is missing data?

Answer: Gaps in a dataset where information was not recorded. Real data almost always has some missing values. Note them and consider how they might affect your conclusions.

3. Why are surprises in data valuable?

Answer: Because surprising findings often lead to the most important discoveries. If data always matched expectations, we would never learn anything new.

4. How should you report unexpected findings?

Answer: Honestly! State what you expected, what you found, and why the difference matters. Never hide findings just because they surprised you.
