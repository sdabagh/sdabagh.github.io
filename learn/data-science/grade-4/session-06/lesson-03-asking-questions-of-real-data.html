Check Your Understanding

1. How do you ask a question of a dataset?

Answer: Start with curiosity, then check: does this dataset have the information I need to answer my question? If yes, figure out which columns to look at and what calculations to do.

2. What if the dataset does not answer your question?

Answer: You might need a different dataset, or you might need to collect additional data. Not every question can be answered by every dataset.

3. What types of questions work well with real data?

Answer: 'What is the highest/lowest?' 'What is the average?' 'Is there a trend over time?' 'How do two groups compare?' These are all answerable with data.

4. How do you know if your answer is correct?

Answer: Check your calculations, verify against the source, and see if the answer makes sense. If the average temperature in July seems to be -10°F, something is wrong.
