Session 7 Family Guide

Data Science for Young Minds — Tips for families facilitating Session 7: What Did We Find Out?

Overview

Look at your pictograph and answer questions. Learn to read your own data and tell others what you found.

Facilitating Each Lesson

Lesson 1: Asking Questions About Data

Tip: Your pictograph answers questions! Learn to ask: 'Which has the most? The fewest? How many more?' Take time to let your child explore and share their thoughts.
Try asking: "What do you think about asking questions that data can answer?"

Lesson 2: Finding the Answer in the Data

Tip: Practice pointing to your pictograph and answering questions. The answer is right there in the stickers! Take time to let your child explore and share their thoughts.
Try asking: "What do you think about finding answers by reading pictographs?"

Lesson 3: Saying What You Found

Tip: Practice the sentence: 'We found out that...' Tell others what your data shows using clear words. Take time to let your child explore and share their thoughts.
Try asking: "What do you think about using 'we found out that...' to communicate results?"

Lesson 4: Presenting to Someone

Tip: Present your pictograph to a family member. Explain what you asked, what you found, and what it means. Take time to let your child explore and share their thoughts.
Try asking: "What do you think about presenting data findings to an audience?"

Conversation Starters

  • "What did you learn about asking questions that data can answer?"
  • "What did you learn about finding answers by reading pictographs?"
  • "What did you learn about using 'we found out that...' to communicate results?"
  • "What did you learn about presenting data findings to an audience?"
