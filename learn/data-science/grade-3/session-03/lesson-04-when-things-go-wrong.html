Check Your Understanding

1. What is a data collection error?

Answer: A mistake made while gathering data — like miscounting, recording the wrong answer, or skipping some people. Small errors can change your results.

2. Why should you write down your method?

Answer: So someone else could repeat your exact steps and get similar results. If your method is unclear, nobody can verify your findings.

3. What happens if you change your question in the middle of a survey?

Answer: Your results become unreliable because some people answered a different question than others. Always keep your question exactly the same.

4. How can you check your data for mistakes?

Answer: Count your total responses (do they match how many people you asked?), look for answers that seem impossible or unusual, and have someone else review your recordings.
