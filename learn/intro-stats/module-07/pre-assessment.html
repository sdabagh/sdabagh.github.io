Module 6 Pre-Assessment

Test your baseline knowledge of confidence intervals

About This Assessment

This pre-assessment helps us understand what you already know about confidence intervals before you start the module. Your responses are for learning purposes only—don't worry if you don't know the answers yet! That's what the lessons are for.

  • 5 questions covering key confidence interval concepts
  • No time limit – take your time
  • Not graded – this is just to measure your starting point
  • You'll take the same assessment after completing the module to measure your learning gains
1 What is a confidence interval?
2 What does a 95% confidence level mean?
3 What is the margin of error in a confidence interval?
4 When should you use the t-distribution instead of the z-distribution for a confidence interval for a mean?
5 What happens to the margin of error if you increase the sample size (all else being equal)?

Remember: This is not graded. It's just to see what you know before starting the module.
