About This Pre-Assessment

This short assessment measures what you already know about two-sample hypothesis testing before you start the module. Don't worry if you don't know the answers—that's expected!

  • 5 questions covering key concepts from Module 9
  • Not graded - This is just for measuring your learning gains
  • ~5 minutes to complete
  • You'll retake the same assessment after completing the module

1Which scenario requires a paired (dependent) samples test?

2In a two-sample t-test for independent samples, what does the null hypothesis typically state?

3When conducting a two-proportion z-test, what is the pooled proportion used for?

4A researcher wants to test if a new teaching method improves test scores. She randomly assigns 30 students to the new method and 30 different students to the traditional method. Which test should she use?

5What is the main advantage of using a paired samples design over an independent samples design?
