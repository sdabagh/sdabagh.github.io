Module 5: Sampling Distributions

Master the Central Limit Theorem and understand how samples behave

4 Lessons
~8 hours
20 Practice Problems
15-Question Quiz

Prerequisites: Modules 2, 3, & 4

This module builds on concepts from Module 2: Descriptive Statistics (mean, standard deviation), Module 3: Probability Basics (probability distributions, expected value), and Module 4: The Normal Distribution (z-scores, normal probabilities). Make sure you're comfortable with these topics before starting.

Review Module 2 Review Module 3 Review Module 4

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

  • Distinguish between parameters and statistics
  • Explain what a sampling distribution is and why it matters
  • Describe the sampling distribution of sample means (x̄)
  • State and apply the Central Limit Theorem
  • Calculate the mean (μₓ̄ = μ) and standard error (σₓ̄ = σ/√n) for sample means
  • Use the normal approximation for sample means and proportions
  • Determine when the Central Limit Theorem applies
  • Calculate probabilities using sampling distributions of means
  • Apply sampling distribution concepts to real-world problems

Study Tips for Module 5

  • Understand the "why": The Central Limit Theorem is powerful—understand why it works, not just how to use it
  • Parameters vs. statistics: Keep the notation straight (μ for population, x̄ for sample)
  • Check conditions: Always verify CLT conditions before applying it (n ≥ 30 or population is normal)
  • Standard error matters: SE = σ/√n shows larger samples give more precise estimates
  • Practice, practice: Sampling distributions can be abstract—work through many examples to build intuition
