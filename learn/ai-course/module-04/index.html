Module 4 of 8

Talk to Claude Like a Person

The art of the follow-up — why the best answers come after the first message.

~20 minutes
📌 Before You Start

What you need:

  • Claude open in another tab at claude.ai
  • A few minutes to have a real back-and-forth conversation

What you’ll do:

Have a real conversation with Claude. Push back. Redirect. Refine. Watch how the answers evolve when you treat it like a dialogue instead of a search box.

💡 The Big Idea

You don’t have to get it right on the first try.

Claude remembers everything in your conversation. You can come back to anything it said. You can push back. You can redirect entirely.

Things you can say to Claude mid-conversation:

That’s not what I meant — [clarify] Make it shorter Explain that last part differently Start over with a different approach Go deeper on that second point Give me a concrete example Actually, I’m asking for a different reason — [explain]

This is a conversation, not a search engine.

The biggest mistake people make: they send one prompt, get a mediocre answer, and give up. The magic is in the follow-up. Almost always.

👀 See It

Here’s a conversation that evolves step by step into something genuinely useful:

You
What’s a good way to stay focused when working from home?
Claude
Here are some strategies: set designated work hours, create a dedicated workspace, use the Pomodoro technique (25 minutes on, 5 off), minimize phone notifications, and plan your day the night before...
You (follow-up)
Actually I meant something different — I struggle with getting started on hard tasks, not staying focused once I’m in it.
Claude (adjusted)
That’s a really different problem — task initiation, not focus maintenance. Some approaches that help: commit to just 2 minutes of the task (you can stop after), use body doubling (work near another person), or write down exactly what “starting” looks like before you sit down...
You (follow-up)
Can you make that into a short checklist I could print and put on my desk?
Claude (final)
✓ Write down the first physical action of the task before you start
✓ Set a 2-minute timer — you only have to do 2 minutes
✓ Find a body double if possible (person nearby, video call, library)
✓ Remove visible distractions from your desk first
✓ Say out loud what you’re about to do

The first answer was fine. The final answer was actually useful — because of two follow-ups.

🖐️ Try It

You’re going to have a conversation that evolves. Follow these steps exactly.

  1. 1
    Start a new conversation in Claude. Copy this in:
    Copy this into Claude 👇
    Give me ideas for a healthy breakfast.
  2. 2
    Read what Claude gives you. Now add a constraint:
    Copy this into Claude 👇
    I don't eat eggs or dairy. Update your suggestions.
  3. 3
    Notice how Claude adapts. Now narrow it further:
    Copy this into Claude 👇
    I have 5 minutes in the morning. Which of these is fastest to prepare?
  4. 4
    One more step — make it practical:
    Copy this into Claude 👇
    Write me a simple grocery list for that breakfast. Just the ingredients, nothing else.
  5. 5
    Look at what you have now. You started with a vague request and ended with a personalized grocery list — through conversation. That is the skill.
🛑 You can stop here if you need a break. The Brain Break and Wrap Up are short — come back when you’re ready.
🧠 Brain Break

Stand up if you can. Shake your hands out. You’re doing great — this is real learning happening.

Stand up Shake out your hands Roll your shoulders Take a breath

Your brain literally needs movement to consolidate what it just learned. This is not filler.

✅ Wrap Up

The ONE thing to remember from this module:

Claude is a conversation partner. Follow up, redirect, push back. The best answers come after the first message.

If you ever get a mediocre answer, don’t start over. Just respond with a follow-up. “That’s not quite what I needed —” is a completely valid next message.
