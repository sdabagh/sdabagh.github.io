Check Your Understanding

1. What is Zakat?

Answer: Zakat is obligatory charity in Islam — one of the five pillars. Muslims who meet a minimum wealth threshold must give 2.5% of their savings annually to those in need.

2. What is Sadaqah?

Answer: Sadaqah is voluntary charitable giving. Unlike Zakat, there is no required amount or time. It can be money, time, kindness, or any act of generosity.

3. What is the difference between Zakat and Sadaqah?

Answer: Zakat is obligatory and has specific rules about who gives, how much, and who receives. Sadaqah is voluntary and can be any amount at any time to anyone in need.

4. Why is giving considered a financial responsibility in Islam?

Answer: Because wealth is a trust (Amana). Part of being a good steward is sharing with those who have less. Giving purifies wealth and strengthens the community.
