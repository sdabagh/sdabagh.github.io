📚 Key Vocabulary
Zakat (زكاة) — obligatory charitable giving, 2.5% of eligible wealth per year
Sadaqah (صدقة) — voluntary charity, any act of generosity
Waqf (وقف) — a charitable endowment; assets donated permanently for community benefit
Barakah (بركة) — divine blessing; the idea that giving multiplies wealth spiritually
Nisab — the minimum amount of wealth that makes Zakat obligatory
Giving budget line — a planned portion of income set aside for charity
Financial plan — a personal roadmap for earning, spending, saving, and giving
🧠 ND-Friendly Teaching Tips
  • Celebrate explicitly — tell students what they've accomplished. Many ND kids thrive on specific, named recognition.
  • Reflection hook — give 2 full minutes of quiet writing before asking anyone to share. No cold-calling.
  • Financial plan — use simple prompts on board. One section at a time. Don't release all 5 sections at once.
  • Zakat math — anchor with $1,000 example: $1,000 × 0.025 = $25. Use round numbers only.
  • Journal time — frame as "for yourself, not to be graded." Some students write more when they feel safe.
  • Closure energy — the one-word wrap-up works well for students who get overwhelmed by big speeches. Low stakes, high participation.
  • Certificates/celebration — even symbolic recognition (verbal, sticker, printed card) matters enormously.
💬 Discussion Questions + Teacher Notes
  • "Look at your Session 1 journal — what did you write? How has your thinking changed?"
    → Students may not remember. Give them a minute to actually look at it. Powerful anchor for the full arc.
  • "Why would a religion make giving a financial obligation?"
    → Community stability, wealth circulation, prevents hoarding. Economic function AND spiritual one.
  • "Is $2.50 out of every $100 a lot or a little?"
    → Leads to: over time, in aggregate, this becomes massive community resources. Scale matters.
  • "What's the difference between Zakat and Sadaqah?"
    → Zakat = obligatory (like a duty), fixed rate, specific recipients. Sadaqah = anything, anytime, any amount.
  • "If giving grows your money (Barakah), why doesn't everyone just give more?"
    → Great values discussion. Trust, patience, believing in long-term thinking. Connects to Session 4.
  • "What is ONE habit you'll take from this course?"
    → Use for closing discussion. Invite 4–5 students. Accept any answer — there's no wrong answer here.
🗺️ My Financial Plan — Activity Guide
Students fill out their own 5-section financial plan. Use real (or estimated) numbers. Encourage honesty — this is for them, not graded.
⚡ Zakat Math Anchor:
2.5% = 2.5 ÷ 100 = 0.025
$1,000 savings → $1,000 × 0.025 = $25
$500 savings → $500 × 0.025 = $12.50
Write both on the board before the activity.
Section 1 — My Money In: Where does your money come from? (allowance, chores, gifts, etc.)
Section 2 — My Spending Plan: What do you regularly spend on? What do you want to cut?
Section 3 — My Savings Goal: What are you saving for? How much per week? How long?
Section 4 — My Giving Line: How much will you give? Zakat-style (2.5%) or set amount?
Section 5 — My 1-Year Goal: One financial goal to achieve in the next 12 months.
💡 After 25 min, ask 2–3 students to share one section. Celebrate specificity: "I love that you wrote an exact number."
🔁 Reflection Hook
"Open your Session 1 worksheet. Look at your journal answer. What did you write about money? Read it — does it still feel true?"
Give 2 min quiet reflection. 2–3 volunteers share. Then: "Today we close the loop."
→ If they don't have S1 worksheet, ask: "What did you think about money before this course?"
✍️ Final Journal Prompt
Write on board:
"What is one financial habit you want to start because of this course? What will you do differently with money starting this week?"
10 min. Private writing. 2–3 share if comfortable.
🌟 Closing Celebration
One-Word Wrap-Up:
"Go around — one word for what money means to you NOW, after 8 sessions."
Write words on board as students say them.
Final message:
"You've done something most adults never do — you've thought carefully about money AND values together. That's rare. You're already ahead."
