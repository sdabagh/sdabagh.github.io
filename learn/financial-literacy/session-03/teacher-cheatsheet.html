📚 Key Vocabulary
Income — money coming IN (allowance, earnings, gifts)
Expense — money going OUT (anything you spend on)
Budget — a plan for how to use your money
Surplus — income > expenses = money left over! ✅
Deficit — expenses > income = you spent too much ⚠️
Sadaqah (صدقة) — voluntary charity; a deliberate budget line
🧠 ND-Friendly Teaching Tips
  • Budget visually — draw 3 buckets on the board (Needs / Wants / Savings+Giving). Students can see money "going in."
  • Use their scale — $10, $20, $50. Not adult salaries. Their numbers = their engagement.
  • Budget Challenge — tactile learners: use play money or chips if available. Moving = understanding.
  • Deficit/surplus — write BIG on board as visual anchors. Deficit = red, Surplus = green.
  • Sadaqah line — frame as "giving has a plan, just like spending does" — not guilt-based.
  • Pause after each term — "Can someone give me an example from their life?"
  • Warn before budget challenge ends — "2 more minutes!"
💬 Discussion Questions + Teacher Notes
  • "What's the difference between how much you get and how much you spend?"
    → Let them invent the words "income" and "expenses" before you give them. Validates their thinking.
  • "Has anyone ever run out of money before the end of the week?"
    → Normalize this. Deficit happens to everyone. Budget prevents it. Don't shame.
  • "What should you do if your expenses are more than your income?"
    → Earn more OR spend less. Both are valid. Or both at once!
  • "Why would someone set aside money for charity BEFORE spending on wants?"
    → Sadaqah as priority, not afterthought. Islamic practice: give first. Discuss without pressure.
  • "What's an unexpected expense that could wreck a budget?"
    → Sick pet, broken phone, friend's birthday. Builds empathy + emergency fund concept early.
  • "Is it better to have a strict budget or a flexible one?"
    → Depends on personality. Rigid budgets fail; flexible budgets need discipline. Both sides valid.
🎮 Budget Challenge Activity Setup
Setup: Each student/group gets $200/month income to allocate on a budget sheet.
Fixed Costs (must pay — write on board):
Lunch $40 · Phone plan $20 · Bus pass $15 = $75 fixed → $125 to allocate freely
Category Options (students choose amounts):
Clothes Entertainment Savings Sadaqah Snacks Hobbies Emergency Fund
⚡ Twist (halfway through): "Your phone screen cracked! Repair = $30. Adjust your budget — where does it come from?"
Debrief:
  • Where did you cut? How did it feel?
  • Did anyone budget for emergencies already?
  • What would you do differently?
🎯 Opening Hook
"Imagine I give you $50 right now. What's the first thing you'd buy?"
(Take 3–4 answers)
"What if I gave you $50 EVERY week for a month? Would you spend it the same way?"
"A budget is just a plan. Like a map for your money. Without it, you wander."
✍️ Journal Prompt
Write on the board:
"If I had $100 every week, how would I split it?"
8 min quiet writing. Encourage them to write actual dollar amounts.
If stuck: "Start with what you NEED to pay for each week."
👋 Close + Preview
Recap:
"One thing you'd budget differently now?"
Preview Session 4:
"Next week: SAVING — how to keep money and how to make it grow. Think about something you're saving toward."
Take-home: Ask your family: "Do we have a budget? How does it work?"
