Course Description

This course introduces statistical thinking and quantitative reasoning. Students learn to collect, organize, analyze, and interpret data using both descriptive and inferential statistical methods.

Topics Covered

  • Data collection and sampling methods
  • Descriptive statistics (measures of center, spread, position)
  • Data visualization and graphical displays
  • Probability basics and distributions
  • Normal distribution and Central Limit Theorem
  • Confidence intervals
  • Hypothesis testing (one and two samples)
  • Correlation and regression
  • Chi-square tests

Student Success

Tips for Success in Statistics

  • Stay current: Statistics builds on itself—don't fall behind
  • Practice regularly: Work through practice problems, not just watch solutions
  • Ask questions: If you're confused, others probably are too
  • Use office hours: I'm here to help—take advantage of support
  • Form study groups: Explaining concepts to peers deepens understanding
  • Connect to real world: Think about how statistics applies to your life and interests
